Key Information

  • 3 blocks of rental-only flats (265 units total)
  • Building heights: 15, 12, and 10 storeys
  • Two blocks will be just 20 meters from Queenshurst
  • Significant impact on sunlight and living conditions for Queenshurst residents

Main Concerns

  1. Height and Proximity: The proposed buildings are approximately twice the height of Queenshurst and extremely close (20m)
  2. Loss of Sunlight: Substantial reduction in sunlight for Queenshurst residents, especially Core 4
  3. Overdevelopment: The proposal exceeds the original Kingston brief which specified max 12 storeys on 20% of site
  4. Garden Location: Poorly planned garden placement next to traffic on Sopwith Way

Alternative Solutions We're Proposing

  • Relocate the gardens between Queenshurst and the new development
  • Move the two nearest blocks further south towards Sopwith Way
  • Reduce heights of blocks near Queenshurst to 8 storeys
  • Maintain the bike/pedestrian route as per original council brief

How to Submit Your Objection

Your voice matters! Even a brief objection can make a difference. Focus on these key points:

  • Building height and proximity to existing buildings
  • Loss of sunlight and impact on quality of life
  • Overdevelopment of the area
  • Traffic concerns on Seven Kings Way
